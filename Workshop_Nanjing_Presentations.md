# Nanjing Paleoclimate Modelling Workshop 2026

## Presentation Principles and Structure

There is no "prescribed" structure for scientific talks, but the following is set of principles, and a possible structure:
* A logical structure makes a presentation much easier to follow for the audience
* A strong initial background and motivation will help keep your audience engaged
* Large figures with clear axes labels mean that the audience can see your results
* Having succinct text means that the audience will listen to you, rather than read your slides.  Too much text can be distracting
* Looking at the audience will help to keep their attention

With the above principles in mind, a possible structure, that I often aim to follow myself, is:
(1) Title slide with contents of talk
(2) Motivation and Previous Work
(3) Research Questions / Hypotheses
(4) Methods
(5) Results and Analysis
(6) Conclusions
(7) Future Work
