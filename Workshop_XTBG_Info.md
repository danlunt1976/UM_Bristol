# XTBG Paleoclimate Modelling Workshop 2026

## Professor Dan Lunt, Professor Shufeng Li

This workshop will provide an introduction to climate models and paleoclimate modelling. In particular, you will have an opportunity to explore paleoclimate model outputs, with a focus on visualisation, model-data comparison, and analysis of mechanisms. You will have the opportunity to explore cutting-edge simulations that have not previously been published or analysed in detail, and to apply them to paleoclimate problems of your own choosing.  During the seminar session, you will present your new work to the group. 

The workshop is organised into 3 sessions, running over a total of 4 days, from **10m on Tuesday 2nd June 2026 to 5:30pm on Friday 5th June 2026:**

**Session 1: Introduction to climate models and paleoclimate modelling**
* Welcome – Prof Li
* Introduction to climate - Prof Lunt
* Introduction to climate models – Prof Lunt
* Introduction to paleoclimate modelling  - Prof Lunt

**Session 2: Visualisation and analysis of paleoclimate model outputs**
* Practical exercise 1: Paleo Modelling Handbook 
	* [Coding Handbook](Paleo_Modelling_Handbook_coding.html)
	* [Exercise Handbook](Paleo_Modelling_Handbook_core.html)

**Session 3: DIY paleoclimate modelling**
* Introduction to University of Bristol Paleo Modelling Group simulation database – Prof Lunt
	* [Info on Database](Paleo_Modelling_Handbook_extra)
* Practical exercise 2: Project work on paleoclimate periods/questions of your choice
	* [Guidance](Workshop_Nanjing_Exercise.md)

**Session 4: Seminar**
* Presentations from individuals or groups on their findings from their personal projects.
	* [Guidance for Presentations and Seminar Timetable](Workshop_Nanjing_Presentations)

**Requirements:**

* Your own laptop
* Python and Jupyter notebook installed on your laptop

**Preparation:**

No preparation is necessary, but if you like then you could read two articles in advance of the meeting, which are supposed to be a fun introduction to climate modelling, based on the movies/books [Lord of the Rings](https://www.paleo.bristol.ac.uk/~ggdjl/reports/middle_earth_1.4.pdf) and [Game of Thrones](https://www.paleo.bristol.ac.uk/~ggdjl/westeros/game_thrones_1.0.pdf).

If you are really keen then you could also read two papers which describe some of our existing simulations form the [Phanerozoic](https://doi.org/10.5194/cp-17-1483-2021) and [Last Glacial Cycle](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.quascirev.2009.10.011).

And if you are mega-keen then you could also read a [description of our climate model](https://doi.org/10.5194/gmd-10-3715-2017).
